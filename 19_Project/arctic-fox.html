Fur colour

Arctic foxes change their fur colour according to the season. In the winter they have white fur while in the summer - brown.

Diet

Lemmings are their main diet. But they would eat anything that is avaliable in the frozen tundra due to the harash enviroment.

Caring for babies

Both father and mather stay together to raise their children. Father's job is to hunt food and keep away predators.

Partnes

Arctic foxed are monogamous animals and have only one partner.

Lifespan

Usually from 3 to 4 years in the wild and up to 14 years in captivity.

Habitat and Range

They live in the arctic and can found in Canada, Greenland, Iceland, nothon parts of Russia and Scandinavia.

Natural enemies

Wolves, Goldon eagles, and Polar bears mainly. Youngsters are not always safe in the den sice they could be dug out

Breeding

Arctic foxes give birth usually in the spring but they start searching for partner in the winter.

Pregnancy and birhs

The gestation period lasts between 50-58 days. Usually they have litters of 5 to 8 but may be even more than 20.

Apperance in seasons

Despite fur colour arctic foxes look thinner in the Summer and fatter in the winter due to the thickness of their fur.

Hunting

Excellent hearing allows them to track their pray. They jumps high and dive headfirst into the snow.

Unberable temperature

Due to unique system of heat exchange and thick fur, the arctic fox won't shiver unless temperature drops below −70 °C (−94 °F).

eyesight

Arctic foxes have excellent sense of smell and hearing but poor sight.

Size

Adult males weight averagely 3.5 kg (7.7 lb) while and females - 2.9 kg (6.4 lb). Small size makes them pray to other animals.

Hunted by humans

Arctic foxes are hunted for their fur.It takes about 20 foxes to make one coat.
